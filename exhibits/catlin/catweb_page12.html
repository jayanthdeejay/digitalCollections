Medicine Painter: George Catlin on the Upper Missouri, 1832.  (Narrative: Page 12)

Buffalo’s Back Fat (Stu-mick-o-sucks) (Kainah). Plate 11. Catlin. The Manners, Customs and Condition of the North American Indians. London, 1892. Archives & Rare Books Department, University Libraries, University of Cincinnati.

The Six (Sha-co-pay) (Plains Ojibwa). Plate 35. Catlin. The Manners, Customs and Condition of the North American Indians. London, 1892. Archives & Rare Books Department, University Libraries, University of Cincinnati.

Among the portraits Catlin executed at Fort Union are two of his best, both from a documentary and artistic perspective. Buffalo Bull’s Back Fat, Head Chief of the Blood or Kainah branch of the Blackfeet Nation, was also painted the next year by Karl Bodmer who accompanied Maximilian zu Wied on his Upper Missouri Expedition. The Six was a Plains Ojibwa who had been painted years earlier by Charles Bird King during a visit to Washington, D.C.
